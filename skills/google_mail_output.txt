


Gmail
Email Composition

Write clear, descriptive subject lines in bold mental priority
Use professional greetings: "Dear [Name]" or "Hello [Name]"
Bold important action items or deadlines in the email body
Use bullet points for multiple items or questions
Highlight urgent items in red or bold
Add a professional signature with your contact details (Settings > Signature)

Organization

Use labels with color coding: Red (urgent), Yellow (pending), Green (completed), Blue (information)
Star important emails for quick access
Create filters to auto-organize incoming mail (Settings > Filters)
Use categories/tabs to separate Primary, Social, Promotions
Archive emails instead of deleting to keep records
Snooze emails that need attention later

Productivity

Use canned responses/templates for frequent emails (Settings > Advanced > Templates)
Enable keyboard shortcuts for faster navigation (Settings > Keyboard shortcuts)
Schedule emails to send at optimal times (click arrow next to Send)
Use @mentions in emails to highlight specific recipients
Set up vacation responder when out of office (Settings > Vacation responder)

Best Practices

Proofread before sending
Keep emails concise (under 5 sentences when possible)
Use CC sparingly (only those who need to know)
Reply within 24 hours to maintain professionalism
Use read receipts for important emails only
